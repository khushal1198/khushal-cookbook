# Jain Paneer Tikka Masala (No Onion, No Garlic, High Protein)

Hybrid of three Jain paneer recipes, restaurant-style. The body comes from dudhi + cashew (V1/V4), the savory depth from hing in the tadka (V3/V4), the protein boost from blended cottage cheese in the gravy + Greek yogurt at the finish, and the "tikka" character from marinating and charring the paneer (V4).

Roughly **20–25 g protein per serving** with 300 g paneer.

## Ingredients

### Cashew–dudhi paste (the body)
- Cashews — 10–12
- Watermelon seeds (charmagaz) — 1 tbsp
- Dudhi (bottle gourd), peeled and chopped — 1 cup
- Saunf (fennel seeds) — 1 tsp
- Cumin seeds — 1/2 tsp
- Green chili — 1, chopped
- Ginger — 1 inch, chopped
- Milk — 4 tbsp (for grinding)

### Tomato puree
- Medium tomatoes — 3, pureed and **strained**

### Paneer marinade (~30 min, ideally 1 hr)
- Paneer, cubed — 300 g, pricked all over with a fork
- Curd (warmed) — 3 tbsp
- Wheat flour — 1 tsp
- Corn flour — 1 tsp (or maida or besan)
- Garam masala — 1 tsp
- Amchur (dry mango) powder — 1 tsp
- Red chili powder — 1 tsp
- Turmeric — 1 tsp
- Chaat masala — 1 tsp
- Oil — 1 tbsp
- Salt — to taste

### Gravy tadka
- Oil or ghee — 3 tbsp
- Cinnamon stick — 1 inch
- Cloves — 3–4
- Green cardamom — 4–5
- Black peppercorns — 10–12
- Cumin seeds — 1/2 tsp
- Hing (asafoetida) — 1 tsp

### Ground spices + protein add
- Coriander powder — 1 tsp
- Red chili powder — 1 tsp (use Kashmiri for color)
- Turmeric — 1/2 tsp
- Sonth (dry ginger) powder — 1 tsp
- Salt — to taste
- Sugar — 1 tsp
- Cottage cheese, blended smooth — 100 g (for protein + body)

### Finish
- Tomato ketchup — 1 tbsp (optional; restaurant "secret" — adds depth + color)
- Garam masala — 1 tsp
- Kasuri methi — 1/2 tsp, crushed between palms
- Greek yogurt (whisked smooth) — 50 g (off heat, for protein + tang)
- Cream — 2 tbsp
- Coriander leaves — chopped, to garnish
- Lemon — wedge to squeeze at serve
- Grated paneer — 1 tbsp, to garnish (optional restaurant move)

## Steps

### 1. Marinate paneer
1. Prick paneer cubes on every side with a fork — this lets the marinade actually flavor the inside.
2. Whisk warmed curd with wheat flour + corn flour smooth. Add garam masala, amchur, red chili, turmeric, chaat masala, salt, and 1 tbsp oil. Mix.
3. Coat paneer cubes thoroughly. Refrigerate 30 min minimum (1 hr ideal).

### 2. Prep the bases
4. Puree the 3 tomatoes and **strain through a sieve** — discard pulp/seeds. The strained puree is what gives the silky restaurant texture.
5. Grind cashews + watermelon seeds + dudhi + saunf + cumin + green chili + ginger + milk to a **very smooth** paste (no graininess).
6. Blend the cottage cheese with a stick blender until completely smooth (no curd flecks).

### 3. Build the gravy
7. Heat 3 tbsp oil/ghee in a pan. Add cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, peppercorns, and cumin seeds. Sauté 30 seconds.
8. Add hing. Stir for 5 seconds.
9. Pour in the strained tomato puree. Add coriander powder, red chili, turmeric, salt. Cook 4–5 min until reduced and oil starts to separate.
10. Add the cashew–dudhi paste. Mix well. Cover and cook 5–6 min on medium-low until oil separates again.
11. Stir in the blended cottage cheese. Mix until fully incorporated and the gravy looks creamy.
12. Add sonth, sugar, and ~1/2 cup water to adjust consistency. Simmer 3–4 min.

### 4. Char the paneer
13. Heat a non-stick pan. Brush with ghee. On high flame, roast the marinated paneer cubes until golden brown on every side. Don't skip this — it's what makes it tikka masala, not butter masala.

### 5. Bring it together
14. Add the charred paneer to the gravy. Stir gently.
15. Add ketchup (if using), garam masala, and crushed kasuri methi. Cover and simmer 2–3 min on low.
16. **Turn off heat.** Whisk Greek yogurt smooth and fold it in (off heat — prevents splitting). Stir in cream.
17. Garnish: grated paneer + chopped coriander + a squeeze of lemon.

### 6. Rest (optional but recommended)
18. If you can, let it sit 30–60 min before serving. Flavors meld noticeably.

## Notes

- **Strained tomato puree is non-negotiable** for the smooth restaurant texture.
- **Fork-prick the paneer** before marinating — the marinade only flavors the surface otherwise.
- **Greek yogurt goes in off heat.** It will split if added to a boiling gravy.
- **Cottage cheese must be fully blended** — any curd flecks ruin the smoothness.
- **No cream + paneer + kasuri methi + yogurt** if you're going to freeze this — see the [freezer gravy base recipe](jain-paneer-tikka-gravy-base-freezer.md).
- Protein math (full batch): 300 g paneer (~54 g) + 100 g cottage cheese (~12 g) + 50 g Greek yogurt (~5 g) + cashews/seeds (~5 g) ≈ **75–80 g protein**, ~20–25 g per serving for 3–4 servings.
