# Jain Paneer Tikka Gravy Base (Freezer Batch)

A 4x batch of the [Jain Paneer Tikka Masala](jain-paneer-tikka-masala.md) gravy, designed to freeze in silicone molds and reuse on weeknights. Cottage cheese is cooked into the base (stable when frozen). Cream, Greek yogurt, kasuri methi, garam masala, and the paneer itself are added **fresh** at serve time.

Yields ~4 batches of gravy. Each frozen puck makes one full sabzi when paired with fresh paneer + finish ingredients.

## Ingredients (4x batch)

### Cashew–dudhi paste
- Cashews — 40–50 (~80 g)
- Watermelon seeds (charmagaz) — 4 tbsp
- Dudhi, peeled and chopped — 4 cups
- Saunf — 4 tsp
- Cumin seeds — 2 tsp
- Green chili — 4
- Ginger — 4 inches
- Milk — 1 cup (for grinding)

### Tomato puree
- Medium tomatoes — 12, pureed and **strained**

### Tadka
- Oil or ghee — 3/4 cup (~12 tbsp)
- Cinnamon stick — 4 inches
- Cloves — 12–16
- Green cardamom — 16–20
- Black peppercorns — ~40
- Cumin seeds — 2 tsp
- Hing — 4 tsp

### Ground spices + protein
- Coriander powder — 4 tsp
- Kashmiri red chili powder — 4 tsp
- Turmeric — 2 tsp
- Sonth (dry ginger) — 4 tsp
- Salt — to taste
- Sugar — 4 tsp
- Cottage cheese, blended smooth — 400 g
- Tomato ketchup — 4 tbsp (optional; restaurant secret)

**Intentionally NOT in the base** (add fresh at serve):
- Cream / Greek yogurt — split on freeze/thaw
- Kasuri methi / garam masala — volatile aromas fade
- Paneer — freezes rubbery

## Steps

### 1. Prep
1. Puree all 12 tomatoes and **strain through a sieve** — discard pulp/seeds.
2. Grind all the cashew–dudhi paste ingredients in batches until very smooth.
3. Blend cottage cheese with a stick blender until no curd flecks remain.

### 2. Build the gravy
4. In a large heavy-bottomed pot, heat the oil/ghee. Add cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, peppercorns, cumin seeds. Sauté ~30 seconds until aromatic.
5. Add hing. Stir 5 seconds.
6. Add strained tomato puree + coriander, red chili, turmeric, salt. Cook 8–10 min on medium until thickened and oil starts to separate.
7. Add the cashew–dudhi paste. Mix well. Cover and cook 10–12 min on medium-low, stirring occasionally, until oil separates again.
8. Stir in blended cottage cheese. Mix until smooth and creamy.
9. Add sonth, sugar, and ketchup. Simmer 5 more min.
10. Taste for salt. The gravy should be thick (it'll loosen when you reheat with water).
11. **Cool completely to room temperature.** This is critical for food safety and texture.

### 3. Portion and freeze
12. Pour into silicone muffin molds (~80–100 ml each, makes ~1 serving) **or** silicone soup molds (~250 ml, makes a full batch's worth).
13. Freeze solid (4–6 hr).
14. Pop pucks out, transfer to a labeled freezer ziplock with date. Press air out.
15. **Lasts ~3 months in the freezer.**

## Reheating into a full sabzi (per puck/batch)

For each frozen batch (~1 muffin mold puck per serving, or ~3-4 pucks for a full sabzi):

1. **Marinate paneer** (300 g per full batch): prick with fork, coat in: 3 tbsp warmed curd + 1 tsp each wheat flour, corn flour, garam masala, amchur, red chili, turmeric, chaat masala + salt + 1 tbsp oil. Refrigerate 30 min while gravy thaws.
2. **Thaw gravy** in a pan with a splash of water (~1/4 cup). Heat on low until liquid, then simmer 3–5 min.
3. **Char paneer** on a non-stick pan with ghee on high flame until golden on every side.
4. **Add charred paneer** to the hot gravy. Stir gently.
5. Add **1 tsp garam masala + 1/2 tsp crushed kasuri methi**. Simmer covered 2–3 min.
6. **Off heat:** whisk 50 g Greek yogurt smooth and fold in. Stir in 2 tbsp cream.
7. Garnish: grated paneer + chopped coriander + lemon squeeze.
8. **Rest 15–30 min if you can** — flavor melds significantly.

## Notes

- **Cream goes in fresh, every time.** Pro consensus is no cream in the freezer base — it separates/gets grainy on thaw.
- **Greek yogurt also fresh.** It can split when reheated from frozen.
- **Cottage cheese is frozen-in** because it's already cooked and emulsified into the oil-rich gravy — it stays stable.
- **Cool to room temp before freezing.** Faster freeze = smaller ice crystals = better texture.
- **Silicone molds > rigid containers** for easy pop-out portioning. Muffin tray (~80–100 ml each) = 1 serving per puck. Soup molds (~250 ml) = full sabzi per puck.
- **Don't over-thicken** the base — it'll reduce more on reheat. Slightly thinner on the freeze side is forgiving.
- **Skip the ketchup** in the base if you want to dial it in fresh per batch.
