# Jain Paneer Butter Masala (No Onion, No Garlic)

Restaurant-style paneer butter masala with no onion and no garlic. The trick is using dudhi (bottle gourd) blended with cashews and watermelon seeds as the base — that's what gives the creamy body that onions usually provide. Source: Chef Aanal Kotak.

## Ingredients

### Gravy tadka (base)
- Oil or ghee — 2–3 tbsp
- Cumin seeds — 1 tsp
- Cinnamon sticks — 1–2
- Cloves — 3–4
- Cardamom pods — 4–5
- Whole black pepper — 10–12
- Green chilies, chopped — 1–2
- Ginger, finely chopped or grated — 1 tbsp
- Tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed — 3–4 medium
- Dudhi (bottle gourd), peeled and chopped — 1 cup
- Cashew nuts — 10–12
- Watermelon seeds — 1 tbsp
- Salt — to taste
- Cumin powder — 1 tsp
- Red chili powder — 1 tsp
- Turmeric powder — 1/2 tsp
- Water — as required

### Paneer butter masala
- Butter — 2 tbsp
- Paneer, cubed — 250–300 g
- Salt — to taste
- Cumin powder — 1 tsp
- Coriander powder — 1 tsp
- Red chili powder — 1 tsp
- Sugar — 1 tsp
- Water — as required
- Cream — 2–3 tbsp
- Fresh coriander leaves, chopped — for garnish
- Lemon juice — 1–2 tbsp

## Steps

### Prepare the gravy base

1. **Heat oil/ghee** in a large pan over medium heat.
2. **Whole spices.** Add cumin seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and whole black pepper. Sauté ~1 minute until aromatic.
3. **Green chilies + ginger.** Add and sauté another minute until fragrant.
4. **Tomatoes.** Add and cook until softened and the oil starts to separate.
5. **Dudhi.** Add the chopped bottle gourd and cook a few minutes until it starts softening.
6. **Nuts + seeds.** Add cashews and watermelon seeds. Cook 5 minutes.
7. **Ground spices.** Add salt, cumin powder, red chili powder, and turmeric. Mix well.
8. **Simmer.** Add water to adjust consistency. Cover and simmer 15–20 minutes until the dudhi is completely soft.
9. **Blend.** Let it cool, then blend to a smooth paste.

### Prepare the paneer butter masala

10. **Butter.** Heat 2 tbsp butter in a separate pan over medium heat.
11. **Sauté paneer.** Add cubed paneer and lightly sauté until just starting to brown.
12. **Spices on paneer.** Add salt, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and 1 tsp sugar. Mix to coat.
13. **Add gravy.** Pour the blended gravy paste into the pan. Mix well.
14. **Simmer.** Cook 5–7 minutes so the paneer absorbs the gravy. Adjust consistency with water.
15. **Cream.** Stir in 2–3 tbsp cream and cook another 2–3 minutes.
16. **Finish.** Garnish with chopped coriander and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

## Notes

- **Dudhi + cashew + watermelon seeds** is the key combo replacing the onion base — don't skip the watermelon seeds, they add body.
- Blend the base smooth — any chunks of dudhi will give it away texturally.
- The 1 tsp sugar in the paneer step isn't optional — it balances the tomato acidity the way restaurant versions do.
- The recipe is technically "paneer butter masala," very close to tikka masala — for a tikka twist, char the paneer cubes first under a broiler or on a hot tawa before adding to the gravy.
