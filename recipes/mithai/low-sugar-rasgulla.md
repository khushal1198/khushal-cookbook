# Low-Sugar Paneer Rasgulla

Rasgulla made with store-bought paneer and a monk-fruit sweetener syrup instead of sugar syrup. The technique notes below are the important part — the boil has to be aggressive and the rest has to be long.

## Ingredients

- Store-bought paneer — kneaded smooth and rolled into small/medium balls
- Water — enough to comfortably hold the balls in one layer
- Monk fruit sweetener — to taste (forms the cooking syrup along with water)

## Steps

1. **Get a hard rolling boil first.** Bring water + monk fruit sweetener to a strong rolling boil on high heat *before* adding the balls.
2. **Add balls gently.** Once boiling hard, drop the balls in one by one. Don't crowd — one layer only.
3. **Keep heat high for the first 2 minutes** after adding them — the temperature drops when the cold balls hit the syrup.
4. **Drop to medium-high and cover.** Keep it actively boiling, not simmering. Cover 80–90%, leaving a small gap if you're worried about overflow.
5. **Boil covered for 12–15 minutes.** Don't stir with a spoon — gently swirl the pot once halfway if needed.
   - Small balls: 12 min
   - Medium balls: 15 min
   - Large balls: 18 min max
6. **Rest covered 25–30 minutes** off the heat. The balls keep absorbing syrup and settling during this rest — this step is not optional.

## Notes

- **Don't over-boil.** Past 18 minutes they turn rubbery/tight, especially without real sugar syrup to protect the texture.
- **Doneness test:** take one out, press gently with a spoon — it should feel springy, not hard. If still dense, boil 3 more minutes.
- **Covered vs uncovered:** covered is better. Uncovered means uneven cooking on top, more evaporation (so sweetener concentrates), less puff, and you'll need 2–3 extra minutes.
- **Don't stir** after adding the balls. Swirl the pot if you need to move them.
- **No sugar syrup means a tighter window** between cooked and rubbery — trust the timer.
