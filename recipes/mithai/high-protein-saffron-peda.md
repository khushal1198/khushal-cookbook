# High-Protein Low-Sugar Saffron Peda

A modern take on malai peda — same mithai richness, but with whey protein folded in and Besti (monk-fruit/allulose) instead of sugar. Naked Whey concentrate works best here because it tastes like milk solids, not gym supplement. No onion, no garlic.

## Ingredients

### Saffron bloom
- Warm milk — 1 tbsp (not boiling, just warm)
- Saffron strands — small pinch (~10 strands), lightly crushed

### Peda
- Ghee — 2 tbsp (~28 g)
- Whole milk powder — 100 g
- Milk — 60 g (in addition to the saffron tbsp above)
- Besti sweetener (monk fruit + allulose) — 60 g (~1/3 cup)
- Naked Whey grass-fed concentrate — 30–40 g
- Cardamom powder — a pinch
- Extra milk powder — for dusting if sticky
- Optional: 1 tiny drop rose water at the end, chopped pistachio for garnish

## Steps

1. **Bloom the saffron.** Crush the saffron strands lightly between your fingers and stir them into 1 tbsp of warm (not hot) milk. Let it steep 10–15 minutes — milk should turn deep yellow/orange.
2. **Melt ghee.** In a non-stick or heavy-bottomed pan on low heat, melt 2 tbsp ghee until just warm. Don't let it smoke.
3. **Roast the milk powder.** Add 100 g whole milk powder. Stir gently on low heat for 4–5 minutes until aromatic and just slightly golden. It will look powdery at first — that's normal.
4. **Add milk.** Combine the saffron milk with the remaining 60 g milk and pour the mixture into the pan. Stir continuously on low heat. It will come together quickly, sometimes sticky — keep stirring.
5. **Add sweetener.** Stir in 60 g Besti gradually so it dissolves evenly. Add cardamom powder.
6. **Cut the heat.** Once it's a soft dough-like consistency, turn off the stove. Adding whey to a hot pan turns it chalky.
7. **Fold in whey.** Add 30–40 g Naked Whey gradually, folding gently with residual heat. Whey absorbs moisture *and* gets tacky — if it turns sticky, dust in a little extra milk powder to bring it back to a soft, shapeable dough. If too dry, a teaspoon of milk at a time.
8. **Optional finish.** Add 1 tiny drop of rose water for "mithai shop aroma."
9. **Rest, then shape.** Let the mixture cool 10–15 minutes until comfortably warm. Lightly grease hands with ghee and roll into small pedas. Garnish with a sliver of pistachio or a saffron strand on top. Let them set at room temperature.

## Notes

- **Naked Whey concentrate > isolate** for mithai. Concentrate is creamier and tastes like milk solids; isolate goes drier and risks chalkiness.
- **Saffron rules:** crush before blooming, use real strands (not powder/artificial), bloom in *warm* milk not boiling. A small pinch is plenty — too much tastes medicinal.
- **Whey timing is the #1 thing to get right.** Add it after the heat is off. High heat denatures it and ruins the texture.
- **Sticky vs dry:** whey can make the mixture sticky, not drier as you'd expect. Counter stickiness with a dusting of milk powder, not more whey. Counter dryness with a teaspoon of milk.
- **Pair saffron with cardamom** — the combo masks any residual whey taste and makes low-sugar mithai feel luxurious.
- **Next time: try adding paneer.** This batch came out a bit sticky — crumbled fresh paneer (or mawa) should add structure, cut the stickiness, and give a more traditional milk-solids bite.
